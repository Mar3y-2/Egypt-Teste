Ta'ameya (Egyptian Falafel)

Ta'ameya, often referred to as Egyptian Falafel, distinguishes itself from other regional falafels
by relying primarily on dried fava beans rather than chickpeas, giving it a characteristic green
hue and a lighter, fluffier texture. It is a fundamental breakfast item and an essential street
food in Egypt. Ta'ameya is usually served stuffed inside pita bread with salad and tahini sauce,
and is typically seasoned heavily with fresh herbs like parsley and coriander.

Ingridiants

  • 1 cup dried fava beans, soaked overnight
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro (كزبرة)
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley (بقدونس)
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • Baking soda and salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying

Recipe

  1. Prepare Mix: Drain the soaked fava beans completely. Place the beans, onion, garlic, cilantro, and parsley in a food processor.
  2. Grind: Process the mixture until it is finely ground but still slightly coarse. You may need to stop and scrape down the sides.
  3. Season: Transfer the mixture to a bowl. Add cumin, coriander, salt, and a pinch of baking soda (just before frying). Mix well.
  4. Shape and Fry: Heat the oil to medium-high heat. Shape the mixture into small, flat patties or balls using your hands or a falafel scooper.
  5. Fry the Ta'ameya until golden brown and crispy on the outside (about 3-5 minutes). Serve immediately with tahini.
