Mahshi

Mahshi is a culinary cornerstone of Egyptian family gatherings and celebrations. It refers to various vegetables—such as zucchini, eggplant, bell peppers, tomatoes, and vine leaves—stuffed with a flavorful mixture of rice, herbs (dill, parsley, coriander), and tomato paste. The dish is slowly simmered in a rich broth until the rice is perfectly cooked and the vegetables are tender. Mahshi is known for its intense aroma and deep, comforting taste.

Ingridiants

  • 1 kg mixed vegetables (zucchini, eggplant, bell peppers, or vine leaves)
  • 2 cups short-grain rice, washed and strained
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup tomato paste or crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup fresh mixed herbs (dill, parsley, coriander), finely chopped
  • Spices: cumin, coriander, cinnamon, salt, and pepper
  • 4 cups chicken or meat broth
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee

Recipe

  1. Prepare Vegetables: Clean and hollow out the vegetables (or prepare vine leaves).
  2. Prepare Stuffing: Sauté the onion in oil. Add tomato paste and spices. Remove from heat, then mix with the washed rice and chopped herbs.
  3. Stuff: Fill the vegetables or roll the leaves with the rice mixture, leaving about a third empty to allow the rice to expand.
  4. Arrange and Cook: Arrange the Mahshi tightly in a large pot. Pour the hot broth until it just covers the top layer of Mahshi.
  5. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat, cover tightly, and simmer for 40–60 minutes until the rice is tender. Serve hot.
