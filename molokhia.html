Molokhia

Almost everyone I know loves Molokhia and it is a mother's secret in Egypt to feed little kids other vegetables
You see, when kids pass their first year, and some people even do it earlier, we introduce the famous rice and Molokhia combo to them.
99.9% of the kids love it, after they get used to it, Egyptian moms start their tricks. Put a pile of rice with Molokhia
and hide anything in there—Broccoli, peas, carrots, beans.... and so on. Just make sure to cut them into small pieces and the kids will gladly
lick their plates. Of course when they get older they will start to object, but at least they got something and you'll feel good about their dinners.

Ingridiants


  • 1 pack frozen molokhia (400g) or 2 cups finely chopped fresh molokhia
  • 5–6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ground coriander
  • 2 tbsp butter or ghee
  • 4 cups chicken broth (or meat broth)
  • Salt & pepper to taste
  • Optional: cooked chicken on the side, rice, or bread

Recipe


  1. Heat the chicken broth in a pot until it starts to simmer gently.
  2. Add the molokhia to the hot broth and stir until fully dissolved. Keep it on low heat and avoid boiling.
  3. In a separate pan, melt the butter or ghee.
  4. Add the minced garlic and ground coriander, and stir until the garlic becomes golden.
  5. Pour the garlic mixture (tasha) into the molokhia pot and mix well.
  6. Add salt and pepper to taste, then let it simmer for 2–3 minutes.
  7. Serve hot with rice, bread, or alongside chicken.
