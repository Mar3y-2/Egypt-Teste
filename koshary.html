Koshary

Koshary is arguably the national dish of Egypt. This hearty and satisfying meal combines various
carbohydrates and proteins into one delicious plate, featuring rice, macaroni, and lentils, all topped
with a rich tomato sauce, chickpeas, and crispy fried onions. Koshary is a staple in Egyptian cuisine
and can be found everywhere, from street carts to high-end restaurants. It's truly a celebration
of simplicity and flavor, offering a cheap, filling, and utterly delicious dining experience.

Ingridiants


  • 1 cup rice
  • 1 cup brown lentils
  • 1 cup macaroni (small elbow or ditali)
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, rinsed
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced (for frying)
  • 3 cups crushed tomatoes
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced (for the sauce)
  • 2 tbsp vinegar
  • Chili powder and cumin to taste

Recipe


  1. Fried Onions: Fry the sliced onions in oil until dark brown and crispy. Set aside, keeping the oil.
  2. Cook Components: Cook rice, lentils, and macaroni separately according to package directions.
  3. Tomato Sauce: In a pot, saut√© half the minced garlic in the onion oil. Add tomatoes, vinegar, cumin, chili, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 20 minutes.
  4. Garlic/Chili Vinegar (Dakka): Mix remaining garlic with water, vinegar, salt, and a dash of chili powder.
  5. Assemble: Layer the rice, then the lentils, then the macaroni.
  6. Top generously with tomato sauce, chickpeas, and finally, the crispy fried onions. Serve hot with the Dakka on the side.
